I started selling digital products in my junior year because I needed rent money and didn't have time for a part-time job. Eight months later, I was making $1,400/month on autopilot while taking 18 credit hours.

Here's exactly how you can do the same, including what sells, where to sell it, and how to create your first product this weekend.

Why Digital Products Work for Students

Digital products have three advantages over every other side hustle:

  1. Create once, sell forever. You put in the work upfront, and the product generates income for months or years with no additional effort.
  2. Zero overhead. No inventory, no shipping, no restocking. Your "warehouse" is a Google Drive folder.
  3. Scalable. One product can sell to 10 people or 10,000 people. Your effort is the same either way.

What Students Actually Buy

Based on my sales data and research from successful student sellers, these five categories consistently perform:

1. Study Guides & Course Notes ($5-$20)

The most obvious and most profitable. If you're in a difficult course with no good free resources online, your notes are worth money.

Best sellers: STEM courses (calculus, organic chemistry, physics), law school outlines, medical school study guides, language learning resources.

Create a clean PDF with your notes, diagrams, practice problems, and answer keys. Price at $9.99-$14.99.

2. Notion Templates ($10-$29)

Notion templates for students are a massive market right now. The most popular templates include:

  • Semester dashboards with assignment trackers
  • Thesis/project management boards
  • Scholarship application trackers
  • Study schedule planners
  • Budget trackers for students

3. Resume & Cover Letter Templates ($5-$15)

Every student needs a resume. Most don't know how to write one. Create 3-5 ATS-friendly templates in Google Docs or Canva, bundle them together, and sell the pack.

4. Academic Planner PDFs ($8-$19)

Printable or digital planners designed for the academic calendar (semester-based, not calendar-year-based). Include monthly spreads, weekly schedules, assignment trackers, grade calculators, and reflection pages.

5. AI Prompt Packs ($7-$17)

This is a new category that's growing fast. Collect and organize the best AI prompts for students:

  • "20 ChatGPT prompts for essay outlining"
  • "10 prompts for exam prep with NotebookLM"
  • "AI prompts for research paper writing"

Where to Sell

  • Gumroad (best for beginners): Free to start, 10% + $0.30 per sale. Handles payment processing and file delivery automatically.
  • Etsy (best for discoverability): $0.20 per listing, 6.5% transaction fee. Etsy brings traffic to you.
  • Your own site (best for margins): Use Shopify or Squarespace. You keep more per sale but need to drive your own traffic.

Start with Gumroad. It's the easiest setup and you can be selling within an hour.

How to Create Your First Product This Weekend

Saturday (2 hours)

  1. Pick one category from the list above
  2. Choose a course you already aced or a system you already use
  3. Open Google Docs or Canva
  4. Create the resource: organize your notes, design your template, write your prompts
  5. Export as PDF

Sunday (1 hour)

  1. Create a Gumroad account
  2. Upload your file
  3. Write a clear product title and description
  4. Set your price (start lower to get initial sales and reviews)
  5. Create a simple product image using Canva

Monday (30 min)

  1. Share your product link in relevant subreddits (r/college, r/students)
  2. Post it in your university's Facebook or Discord groups
  3. Tell classmates directly — word of mouth is your best marketing

What I Earned in My First 90 Days

  • Month 1: $87 (3 sales of a calculus study guide at $14.99)
  • Month 2: $320 (12 sales, added Notion template)
  • Month 3: $680 (28 sales across 4 products)

By month 6, I had 8 products and was averaging $1,200/month. Total ongoing work: maybe 2 hours per month answering questions and updating products.

The Hidden Benefit

Beyond the money, having a digital product portfolio looks great on resumes and in interviews. It shows initiative, entrepreneurial thinking, and the ability to execute. Two of my interviewers mentioned my side business before I did.

Start Today

Most students spend months thinking about starting a side hustle and never actually do it. Pick one product from the list above. Create it this weekend. List it on Gumroad. Even if you only make $50 in your first month, you've learned more about entrepreneurship than any class can teach you.
