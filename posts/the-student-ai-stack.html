Most students are running 8-15 different apps for school. You only need 7, all free, and they integrate into a single pipeline that cuts your weekly study time by 30-40%.

Every student I know is drowning in tabs. ChatGPT for brainstorming, Google Docs for writing, some transcription app for lectures, a flashcard app for exams, Grammarly for editing, Notion for organizing, Google Scholar for research, and a separate PDF reader for readings. That's eight apps before you even open your email.

I tested every combination over two semesters and cut my stack to seven free tools that work together. Here's the exact pipeline.

mic Tier 1

Lecture Capture

Otter.ai (free: 300 min/month). Open it on your phone when class starts. It transcribes in real time, identifies speakers, and generates a summary. You walk out of lecture with searchable text instead of frantic handwritten notes.

Most students don't realize Otter lets you export the transcript and summary as a text file. That file goes straight into Tier 2.

psychology Tier 2

Source-Based Study

Google NotebookLM (free). Upload your Otter transcript, the lecture slides, the assigned reading PDF, and the textbook chapter all into one Notebook. NotebookLM creates an AI model that only answers based on those sources.

Ask it: "Generate five practice questions from today's lecture." Or: "Summarize the three main arguments in this reading and compare them to what the professor said in class." It cannot hallucinate because it only uses your uploaded material.

This is the single highest-leverage tool in the stack. If you only use one tool from this list, make it this one.

travel_explore Tier 3

Research

Perplexity (free). When you need to find sources for a paper, Perplexity searches the web and returns direct answers with inline citations. Every claim links to its source. You can verify anything with one click.

Start every research session here. Ask broad questions, open the cited sources, download the PDFs, and upload them to NotebookLM for deeper analysis.

edit_note Tier 4

Writing

Claude (free tier). Paste your NotebookLM-generated study guide or your research notes into Claude and ask it to help structure your argument. Claude is better than ChatGPT for long-form reasoning and essay structure.

Write your draft in Claude's interface, then export.

spellcheck Tier 5

Editing

Grammarly (free). Run your draft through Grammarly for grammar, clarity, and tone. The free tier catches everything that matters. If your university offers Grammarly Premium through the writing center, grab it — but the free version is enough for most assignments.

memory Tier 6

Memorization

Anki (free, open-source). Upload your NotebookLM-generated Q&As as a flashcard deck. Anki uses spaced repetition: it shows you cards you're about to forget more frequently and cards you know well less often. Research shows this alone improves exam performance by 20-30% compared to passive review.

functions Tier 7

Math & Science

Wolfram Alpha (free). For any STEM course, use Wolfram Alpha to check your work. Enter a calculus problem and it shows every step of the solution. Not a language model guessing — a computational engine that's provably correct.

sync_alt

The Weekly Workflow (2 hours total)

  • After each lecture (10 min): Export Otter transcript → upload to new NotebookLM notebook → ask for summary + practice questions.
  • Weekly review (30 min): Review Anki deck from the week's material. Delete cards you know cold. Keep cards you missed.
  • Paper week (2 hours): Perplexity for research → download PDFs → NotebookLM for analysis → Claude for drafting → Grammarly for polish.
  • Exam prep (1 hour per course): Upload all semester Notebooks into one master Notebook. Ask for comprehensive practice exam. Study flagged Anki cards.

Total cost: $0. Total time saved: 8-12 hours per week. That's your weekends back.

target

The Bottom Line

You don't need the $20/month ChatGPT Plus subscription. You don't need five different note-taking apps. The free tiers of these seven tools, used in sequence, outperform any paid single-tool setup because each one is best at what it does and they pass data to each other naturally.

Stop managing apps and start doing the work.
